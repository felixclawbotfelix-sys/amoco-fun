If you've pulled up to the pump lately and felt a little sticker shock, you're not alone. Gas prices across Illinois — and across the country — have climbed significantly in 2026, and local drivers in Elburn, Geneva, Batavia, and across Kane County are feeling it.

What's Behind the Price Spike?

The biggest driver is the ongoing conflict affecting the Strait of Hormuz — a narrow waterway in the Middle East through which roughly 20% of the world's seaborne oil passes. Disruptions there have sent crude oil prices sharply higher, and those increases flow directly to the rack prices that fuel stations pay wholesale. When wholesale costs rise, retail prices follow.

Illinois drivers are also dealing with some of the highest state fuel taxes in the country. Between state excise taxes, Kane County taxes, and federal levies, every gallon includes over a dollar in taxes before the retailer adds a cent of margin. None of that is changing overnight.
