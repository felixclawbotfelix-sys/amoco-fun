You've probably noticed diesel prices making headlines alongside all the news about artificial intelligence. There's a direct connection — and it affects drivers and businesses in Kane County too.

The "No Lights Out" Policy

Modern AI data centers operate under what the industry calls a "no lights out" policy. Unlike traditional warehouses or office buildings that power down at night, AI data centers never stop. The servers processing ChatGPT queries, training machine learning models, and running cloud infrastructure run continuously — 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, 365 days a year.

The term "lights out" comes from manufacturing — a fully automated facility that can run in the dark because there are no humans inside. AI data centers take this further: no downtime, no maintenance windows that cut power, no nights off.

Diesel Is the Backbone of the Grid

Here's where diesel comes in. Every major data center — from Microsoft to Amazon to Google — maintains massive diesel backup generators. We're talking generators that can power entire city blocks, filled with thousands of gallons of ULSD diesel, ready to kick on the instant grid power fails.

As AI workloads have exploded, so has the number and size of these facilities. The U.S. is projected to add tens of billions of dollars in new data center capacity through 2026 and beyond — all of it requiring diesel backup power.

The result: Diesel demand from data centers alone has become one of the fastest-growing segments of fuel consumption in the country. That demand puts upward pressure on diesel prices at the pump — including right here in Kane County.

What This Means for Local Diesel Drivers
